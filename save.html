Lhosars: The Vibrant Tibetan New Year Celebrations

Lhosar, the Tibetan New Year, is not a single event but a collection of distinct celebrations observed by various Himalayan communities based on their specific lunar calendars. It is a time for family reunions, cleansing, and setting intentions for a prosperous new year.

Gyalpo Lhosar

The Tibetan New Year celebrated by Sherpa, Tamang, and Bhutia communities, following the Tibetan calendar. Discover its rich folk stories, annual dates, and diverse traditions including masked dances and feasting.

Learn More »

Sonam Lhosar

The New Year of the Tamang community, typically falling in February. Explore its unique customs, vibrant cultural programs with traditional Tamang music (Tamang Selo), and guidance on ethnic foods and dress guidance.

Learn More »

Tamu Lhosar

The New Year of the Gurung community, usually celebrated in December. Understand its distinct traditions, ceremonial practices, and festive attire, including traditional Gurung dances like Ghatu and Sorathi.

Learn More »

Other Significant Festivals in Our Calendar

An overview of additional important cultural-religious events that are central to Himalayan spiritual life and celebrated across various communities.

Mani Rimdu

An ancient 19-day festival performed by the monks of Tengboche Monastery. It includes sacred mask dances, prayers, and blessings for peace and good fortune in the Everest region. Usually takes place in late October or early November.

Learn More »

Dumji Festival

A vibrant and deeply spiritual festival celebrated by the Sherpa community, primarily in the Everest region. It marks the birth of Guru Rinpoche and features traditional dances and rituals for prosperity. Celebrated over four days in June or July.

Learn More »

Galdan Namchot

A significant Tibetan Buddhist festival celebrating the birth and parinirvana of Je Tsongkhapa, founder of the Gelug school. Homes and monasteries are lit with butter lamps and candles to dispel darkness. Usually in December.
